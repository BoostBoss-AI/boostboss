Product lifecycle

Every Boost Boss product moves through four states. Knowing where yours sits — and what changes are possible from there — saves you from surprises around the lock rule.

Step 1
Draft
Save anytime. Not yet visible to admins or buyers.
Step 2
Pending audit
In Boost Boss's review queue. Usually approved within 24 hours.
Step 3
Approved — live
Buyers can purchase. Page is locked from edits.
Step 4 (optional)
Changes requested
Admin asked you to update specific items. Edit + resubmit.

The four states in detail

State What it means What you can do
Draft You created the product but haven't submitted it for BB audit. Buyers cannot see it. Admins do not see it in the queue. Edit any field. Click Submit for audit → when ready.
Pending audit Boost Boss is reviewing your product page. Reviews finish within ~24 hours. While pending, the product is read-only — editing it would invalidate the review-in-progress. Wait for the admin decision. You can still preview the live page.
Approved — live Your page is live at boostboss.ai/p/<product>. Buyers can purchase any active plan. Your product is now locked — you can't edit pricing or marketing content. Use Duplicate as new to make changes (see below), or Archive to take it down.
Changes requested The admin reviewed your page and asked you to update specific items before approval. The reviewer notes appear inline at the top of the modal. Edit the requested fields. Click Submit for audit → again to re-queue.
Rejected The page didn't meet the BB acceptable-use rules. You'll get notes inside the modal explaining what tripped the policy. Either rework the page heavily and resubmit, or archive it.

Why approved products are locked

The lock rule is the marketplace-trust anchor. Without it, a seller could pass audit with a clean page, then quietly swap the screenshots, lower the original price, or change the activation URL after buyers have already arrived. That's a bait-and-switch surface BB doesn't tolerate.

The lock makes the audit signal meaningful: when a buyer sees the BB-approved badge, the page they're looking at is the same page the admin actually approved.

Updating a live product

You have two paths:

A. Duplicate as new (recommended)

Best for cases where you want the product to stay live while you prepare the update. No downtime; buyers can still purchase the existing version until the new one is approved.

  1. Open the approved product. Click Duplicate as new at the bottom of the modal.
  2. A fresh draft is created, pre-filled with everything from the original. The new draft has its own ID and starts in Draft status.
  3. Edit the fields that need changing on the new draft.
  4. Click Submit for audit →. BB reviews the new version.
  5. When it's approved, archive the original. The new version takes over — its URL stays at /p/<new-id> but you can swap your share links to point at it.
About affiliate share links: existing bb_click tokens from your share links are tied to the original product's id. After you replace the live version, point your affiliate share links at the new product's id so commissions track correctly.

B. Archive + start over

Use this when the update is so significant that the old version shouldn't keep selling at all (e.g., you're discontinuing a feature, changing the price by >50%, or repositioning the brand).

  1. Open the approved product. Click Archive product at the bottom of the modal.
  2. Confirm. The product disappears from the marketplace immediately. Existing buyers retain access; existing share links keep redirecting (so historical traffic isn't broken), but new buyers see a "no longer available" message.
  3. Click + New product on the products page to start fresh. Fill in the updated version. Submit for audit.

FAQ

Can I un-archive a product?

Yes — open the archived view (filter dropdown on the Products page → Archived), click Restore. The product goes back to its previous audit status (usually Approved). The lock rule still applies.

What if I just need to fix a typo?

Use Duplicate as new. There's no "minor edit" exception to the lock rule by design — the audit signal is binary. Trade-off accepted: a small amount of friction for fixing typos in exchange for the consumer-trust guarantee that an approved page won't quietly mutate.

What about pricing plans?

Plans are audited at the same time as the parent product. Once approved, the plans are also locked. To change prices or add tiers, duplicate the product as above; the new draft inherits the existing plans, and you can adjust them before submitting.

I deleted a product by mistake. Can I recover it?

Hard Delete is permanent — it removes the row plus all its plans. It only works for products with zero recorded orders, so customer-facing damage from an accidental delete is bounded. If you might need to recover later, use Archive instead; archived products can be restored anytime.
