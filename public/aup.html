Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: June 4, 2026 · Effective: June 4, 2026 · Version: 1.0

1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") describes the types of advertising creative, publisher content, and user behavior that are permitted, restricted, and prohibited on the Boost Boss platform. This AUP applies to all users — advertisers, publishers, and visitors — and supplements the Terms of Service.

We reserve the right to interpret and enforce this AUP at our sole discretion. Violations may result in content removal, account suspension, or termination without notice.

2. Permitted Use

✓ Allowed advertiser content

  • B2B SaaS products and technology services
  • AI/ML tools, developer tools, and APIs
  • Educational content and courses
  • Productivity software and consumer applications
  • Hardware and electronics
  • Books, media, and content subscriptions
  • Recruitment for legitimate employment
  • Conferences and events
  • Open source projects and developer communities
  • Cybersecurity products (with verification)
  • Financial services from licensed institutions (with verification)

3. Restricted Categories

The following categories are permitted with conditions. Advertisers in these categories must contact legal@boostboss.ai for pre‑approval and may be subject to additional verification.

⚠ Requires pre‑approval

  • Cryptocurrency and DeFi: only regulated, licensed offerings; no token sales, no unregistered securities
  • Financial products: loans, credit, insurance — must be from licensed providers
  • Healthcare: licensed providers only; no unverified medical claims
  • Supplements and nutraceuticals: compliant labeling and claims required
  • Alcohol: jurisdiction‑appropriate targeting required
  • Gaming and esports: no real‑money gambling or wagering (see Prohibited)
  • Political advertising: requires identity disclosure; subject to applicable election law
  • Dating services: compliant with platform standards
  • Lead generation and affiliate marketing: transparent attribution required

4. Prohibited Categories

The following are strictly prohibited from both advertiser creative and publisher inventory. Accounts that promote, host, or facilitate any of these will be terminated immediately.

✗ Strictly prohibited

  • Illegal goods, services, or activities under applicable law
  • Adult content, pornography, or sexually explicit material
  • Real‑money gambling, wagering, sports betting, or unregulated lotteries
  • Tobacco, e‑cigarettes, vaping products, or smoking cessation with unverified claims
  • Recreational drugs and drug paraphernalia
  • Weapons, ammunition, firearms accessories
  • Counterfeit or trademark‑infringing products
  • Pyramid schemes, multi‑level marketing without product, "get rich quick" schemes
  • Phishing, malware, ransomware, or any malicious software
  • Hate speech, content promoting violence, terrorism, or extremism
  • Content exploiting or endangering minors
  • Misinformation about elections, public health, or science
  • Deepfakes or non‑consensual synthetic media of real people
  • Unregistered securities, ICOs, or token sales
  • Stolen credentials, hacking services, or fraud tutorials
  • Doxxing, harassment, or content threatening individuals
  • Animal abuse or illegal wildlife trade
  • Content from or targeting sanctioned countries (Iran, North Korea, Cuba, Crimea, Syria)

5. Creative Standards

All advertising creative on Boost Boss must meet the following standards:

  • Truthful: claims must be accurate, substantiated, and not misleading
  • Clear identification: ads must be identifiable as advertising; no deceptive disguising as editorial content, system messages, or AI tool responses
  • Functional: ad creatives must load, render correctly, and not interfere with the publisher application
  • Quality: grammatically correct, professionally written, free of broken links
  • Brand‑safe: appropriate for a general professional audience
  • Trademark‑compliant: only use trademarks you own or have license to use
  • Copyright‑compliant: only use creative content you own or have license to use

We may reject or require modification of any creative at our sole discretion.

6. Publisher Standards

Publishers integrating Boost Boss ads into their applications must:

  • Operate a legitimate AI tool, MCP server, app, or service with real user engagement
  • Render ad markup faithfully, without modification, hiding, or repositioning that violates IAB standards
  • Not generate artificial traffic, impressions, or clicks through bots, click farms, incentivized clicks, or auto‑refresh schemes
  • Not misrepresent inventory category, audience size, or geographic distribution
  • Not host or serve ads on content that violates this AUP (e.g., adult content, illegal content)
  • Disclose Boost Boss as their ad partner in a privacy policy or transparency page
  • Comply with applicable consumer disclosure rules (e.g., FTC endorsement guides, GDPR/PIPL consent)
  • Maintain accurate ads.txt or app‑ads.txt records reflecting the Boost Boss seller relationship

6.5 Merchant of Record Products and Audit

Sellers registering products for sale through the Boost Boss-hosted storefront agree to the following content standards. Each Product is reviewed before it appears on the storefront or accrues sales; products that fail audit are returned to draft with notes, and approved Products that later breach these standards may be revoked at any time.

  • Accurate description: the Product title, tagline, feature blocks, hero images, screenshots, and demo content must accurately represent the actual product the buyer will receive. Mock screenshots, unreleased features, or vaporware are prohibited.
  • Honest "original price" comparisons: any displayed strike-through price, list price, or savings claim must be supported by a publicly accessible URL on the seller's own site showing the original price in effect within the prior thirty (30) days. URLs hosted on third-party deal sites or screenshots without a live URL are not accepted.
  • Working fulfilment URL: the fulfilment URL must point at the seller's own signup page, must accept the bb_token query parameter, and must call our voucher API to validate and redeem before granting access. Fulfilment URLs that redirect to an unrelated property, paywall the activation behind an additional purchase, or fail to honour a valid voucher are grounds for revocation.
  • Working refund path: the seller must provide a working refund / support email and honour any refund Boost Boss issues to a buyer under the Refund Policy.
  • Prohibited products: the prohibitions in Section 4 above apply with equal force to storefront Products, including (but not limited to) adult content, illegal goods or services, scam-grade get-rich-quick offerings, MLM enrollments, and unlicensed financial or medical advice.

6.6 Affiliate Standards

Affiliates promoting Boost Boss Products via share-links agree to the following standards. Violations may result in clawback of accrued commissions, suspension of the share-link, and account termination.

  • Disclosure: when sharing a Boost Boss affiliate link on social media, in a newsletter, on a website, in a video, or in any other public or semi-public channel, the affiliate must clearly disclose the affiliate relationship in a manner consistent with applicable consumer protection rules (FTC endorsement guides in the United States; equivalent rules in other jurisdictions). Hashtag-only disclosures (e.g. #ad, #sponsored) are acceptable where the platform supports them.
  • No misleading promotion: affiliates may not misrepresent the Product, fabricate testimonials, claim guaranteed results, or impersonate the seller, Boost Boss, or any third party.
  • No self-dealing: affiliates may not purchase the Product through their own affiliate link to capture the commission, nor coordinate with friends, family, or alternate accounts to do so. Boost Boss reserves the right to clawback any commission whose underlying transaction is reasonably suspected to be self-dealing.
  • No spam: affiliates may not distribute share-links via unsolicited email, SMS, or messaging campaigns; via deceptive search-engine optimization (e.g. cloaking, doorway pages); or via traffic acquired from incentivized-click networks.
  • Trademark caution: affiliates may not bid on the seller's brand keywords in paid search, register lookalike domains, or use the seller's trademarks beyond fair-use reference, unless the seller expressly authorizes them to do so.

7. Fraud and Invalid Traffic

Boost Boss employs automated and manual fraud detection. Suspected invalid traffic (IVT) will be subject to:

  • Withholding of associated publisher payouts pending investigation
  • Clawback of previously paid amounts attributable to invalid traffic
  • Permanent account closure for verified fraud
  • Referral to law enforcement in egregious cases

Sources of invalid traffic include but are not limited to: bots, scripted clicks, click farms, incentivized clicks, hidden ads, auto‑refresh, popunders, malware traffic, and box‑car/click‑injection attacks.

8. Privacy and User Data

Publishers must obtain any consent required by applicable law (GDPR, CCPA, PIPL, etc.) before allowing ad‑serving in jurisdictions that require such consent. Boost Boss provides a basic consent management framework; publishers are ultimately responsible for compliance in their own applications.

9. Reporting Violations

To report a suspected AUP violation, please email legal@boostboss.ai with as much detail as possible. We investigate all reports.

10. Enforcement

Violations of this AUP may result in any combination of:

  • Content removal or campaign pause
  • Account warning
  • Temporary account suspension
  • Permanent account termination
  • Forfeiture of unused balance or unpaid earnings
  • Legal action where applicable

We may take action without prior notice if necessary to protect users or the platform.

11. Changes

We may update this AUP from time to time. Material changes will be announced at least 14 days before they take effect.
