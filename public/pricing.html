FAQ

Questions, answered.

Every placement carries a starting CPM — a price per 1,000 impressions. Your cost for any placement is simply CPM × (impressions ÷ 1,000). A $3.00 CPM placement serving 10,000 impressions costs $30. You only pay for impressions Boost Boss actually delivers.

The per-impression price is the CPM divided by 1,000. Our lowest placement (a Suggested Chip) starts at a $1.50 CPM — that's $0.0015 per impression. The most premium placement, Rewarded Video, starts at a $15.00 CPM ($0.015 per impression). Every placement falls between those two.

No. There are no seats, no minimums, and no monthly platform fee. You fund a campaign, Benna allocates it across the placements you choose, and you're charged only for delivered impressions. Start with $50 or $50,000 — the pricing is identical.

Starting CPMs reflect a placement's prominence and intent. Gentle, in-context units (chips, citations, inline cards) start low because they're lightweight. High-attention formats (interstitials, pre-roll and rewarded video) command higher CPMs because they capture the user's full focus at a high-intent moment. Every price is a floor — competitive bids clear at or above it.

Yes. CPM is the transparent baseline shown here, but campaigns can run on CPC (pay per click-through) or CPA (pay per conversion). Benna optimizes delivery toward whichever goal you set, reallocating spend to the placements converting best for you.

Boost Boss serves ads inside AI products across four surfaces: Browser AI, Browser Extensions, Computer / Desktop apps, and Mobile. Across them are 37 distinct placements — from inline sponsored cards to full-screen interstitials and rewarded video. You can target any subset.

Benna is our optimization engine. Instead of matching cookies, it scores the intent behind each AI interaction and places your ad where a builder is most likely to act. That intent accuracy is why the same budget converts higher here than on display networks — and why you never waste spend on low-intent inventory.

You pre-fund campaign credit (card or PayPal) and spend draws down from it as impressions clear. Every impression is itemized on a transparent receipt — you always see exactly what you paid for. Unused credit stays in your balance.
